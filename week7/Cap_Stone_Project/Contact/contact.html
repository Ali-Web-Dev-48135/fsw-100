We believe that every family has a story. Our museum features an article every month on three selected stories from the hundreds we receive. These stories are selected based on the trials and tribulations and the history attached. Below are afew of the stories we've received and featured.

Sent by Jane Doe, A picture from WW1 of her great grandfather and a fellow marine. The picture was taken 10 minutes before an abmush occured on there base. The picture showcases the young men and woman that fought and protected this country. The age of the picture alone hold it's priceless value.

Sent by John Doe, A picture from WW2 showcasing her great grandmother's military equipment. The equipment was photographed hours before the end of war. The picture has a sense of relief and peace once wars come to an end and normal life is once again restored.

Sent by Sarah Doe, A picture from the great Titanic. The suitcase of her dad's friend is captured during a search upon the sunken ship. The suticase was retrieved and is currently being held by our museum. One of hundred of findings, the photo showcases the belongings of lives that were abort on the ship.
